Instructions: Input a budget of your choosing into the Budget box. Then select a “Flu Season” to look at, as well as a particular region (Northeast, South, or West). Next press “Submit.” After you press submit it will show the budget allocation to each city. Then you can select particular cities in the region to graph the resource allocation model which shows the impact of resources on the number of people infected. The optimal the range for budget you can input for each network is $1M-$12M, $5-$10M, and $9M-$16M for the Northeast, South, and West respectively.

Understanding Results: The Resource Allocation model allocated your inputted budget amongst the city in the specified region. As well as giving the budget breakdown, it also specifies the improved recovery rate in a city as a result of the budget allocation, the natural recovery rates of each city, the number of lives saved per city, and the cost per life saved. When you graph a particular city in the network, the graph shows the number of people infected without (in red) and with (in blue) the resource allocation. The resource allocation occurs at the peak of the average flu season.
